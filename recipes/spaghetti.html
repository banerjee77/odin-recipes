One-Pan Dirty Spaghetti

Description

Spaghetti, and that too one-pan dirty spaghetti is a savoury, 30 minute Southern comfort food that merges 'Cajun' style dirty rice flavours with Italian pasta. It featutes pork, CAjun seasonings and the 'holy trinity'(onion, bell paper and celery), all cooked in one pan with pasta directly in the broth, creating a rich, thick sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
  • 8 onces pork.
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced.
  • 2 cups diced bell paper.
  • 1 cup diced celery.
  • 4 ounces andouille sausage, diced small.
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder.
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder.
  • 1 teaspoon paprika.
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin.
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano.
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme.
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt.
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper.
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce.

Steps to make

  1. Add oil to a deep skillet with a tight fitting lid and set over medium high heat. Add pork and cook, stir for 3-4 minutes, breaking the meat up into small crumbles while doing so.
  2. Once pork is brown, add bell paper, onion, celery and andouille sausage and cook, stirring for 2 more minutes. Season with onion powder, garlic powder, paprika, cumin, oregano, thyme, cayenne, black pepper and salt and cook, stirring for 3-4 minutes more. Pour in broth and let it simmer.
  3. Break spaghetti in half and add to the pan. Distribute spaghetti as evenly as possible and cover the pan tightly. Cook on medium-high for 2 minutes. Uncover and give everything a stir. Repeat this process of uncovering, stirring and covering every 2 minutes or so, until pasta is cooked to your desired doneness.
  4. Turn off the heat and drizzle in Worcestershire sauce, plus another pinch of salt if needed. Stir, to combine, and serve in bowls topped with green onions.
